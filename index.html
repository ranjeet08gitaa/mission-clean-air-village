The Problem

Why Indoor Air Quality Matters

Most rural homes face invisible air threats from cooking smoke, road dust, and crop burning — causing asthma, allergies, and long-term lung damage.

🔥

Cooking Smoke

Chulha and biomass cooking releases PM2.5, carbon monoxide, and toxic fumes directly inside homes — especially harmful for women and children.

🌫️

Road & Construction Dust

Unpaved roads and nearby construction send coarse dust (PM10) into homes constantly. This causes chronic coughing and eye irritation.

🏭

Crop & Waste Burning

Seasonal parali burning and garbage fires create dense smoke that enters homes for weeks, spiking pollution to hazardous levels.

Our Jugaad Solution

How the Wet Scrubber Works

We use a high-pressure air dryer to push dirty air through water. The water traps dust and particles, releasing cleaner air on the other side. Simple physics, real results.

Into Room
1

Air Intake & Compression

The high-pressure air dryer sucks in polluted room air and forces it through a pipe into the bottom of the water tank at high pressure.

Input Stage
2

Water Scrubbing (Bubble Diffuser)

Air exits through a perforated plate with tiny 1-2mm holes, breaking into thousands of small bubbles. Each bubble's surface traps dust and PM particles. The soap in the water reduces surface tension, helping even fine PM2.5 particles stick.

Core Filtration
3

Moisture Separation

Wet air rises to the top and passes through tightly packed steel scrub pads (bartan juna). These catch water droplets and remaining particles, preventing moisture from entering the room.

Drying Stage
4

Final Cloth Filter

Clean air passes through 2-3 layers of damp cotton cloth. This final polish catches any remaining micro-particles. The air that exits is noticeably cleaner and fresher.

Level Up

How to Improve the Design

Start simple, then upgrade step by step as your team gains experience.

Easy

Multi-Chamber Tank

Use 2-3 smaller containers in series instead of one big drum. Air passes through water in each container — triple the filtration, same cost.

Easy

Activated Charcoal Layer

Add a tray of crushed charcoal (koyla) after the water stage. Charcoal absorbs gases, odors, and chemical pollutants that water can't catch.

Medium

Venturi Nozzle Inlet

Narrow the air inlet pipe at the water entry point. This accelerates the air, creating finer bubbles and better particle capture — especially for PM2.5.

Medium

Solar-Powered Pump

Replace the electric air dryer with a small solar-powered air pump. Free energy, silent operation, and perfect for remote villages without stable power.

Advanced

PM2.5 Sensor Monitoring

Attach a cheap PM2.5 sensor (₹300-500 online) at the inlet and outlet. Measure actual particle reduction and share data with the village.

Advanced

Electrostatic Pre-Filter

Add a simple electrostatic stage before the water — charged wires attract fine particles. Dramatically boosts PM2.5 capture to 60-70%.
